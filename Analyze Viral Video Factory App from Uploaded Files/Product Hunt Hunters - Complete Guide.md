# Product Hunt Hunters - Complete Guide

## What is a "Hunter"?

A **Hunter** on Product Hunt is someone who submits/posts products to the platform. Think of them as the "curator" who introduces your product to the Product Hunt community.

### Key Points:
- **You cannot post your own product** - someone else must "hunt" it for you
- **Hunters get credit** for discovering and sharing great products
- **Hunter's reputation matters** - their followers see the products they hunt
- **Hunter's network** can significantly impact your launch success

## Why Hunter Choice Matters

### Impact on Performance:
- **Follower reach**: Top hunters have 10K-100K+ followers who get notified
- **Credibility boost**: Established hunters lend credibility to your product
- **Network effect**: Their connections are more likely to engage
- **Algorithm boost**: PH algorithm may favor products from successful hunters

### Real Example:
- **Unknown hunter**: Product gets 50-200 upvotes
- **Top hunter**: Same product could get 500-1500 upvotes
- **Difference**: Hunter's network and credibility

## Types of Hunters

### 🌟 **Top Hunters** (What you want)
**Characteristics:**
- 10,000+ followers on Product Hunt
- History of hunting successful products (multiple #1s)
- Active in the community with high engagement
- Often entrepreneurs, VCs, or tech influencers themselves

**Examples of Top Hunters:**
- **Ryan Hoover** (PH founder) - 100K+ followers

- **Abadesi Osunsade** - 30K+ followers, focuses on diversity in tech

### 🎯 **Good Hunters** (Solid choice)
**Characteristics:**
- 1,000-10,000 followers
- Regular hunting activity
- Good engagement rates
- Niche expertise (e.g., AI tools, creator economy)

### 📱 **Regular Users** (Avoid for launch)
**Characteristics:**
- <1,000 followers
- Infrequent hunting
- Limited network reach

## How to Find and Approach Top Hunters

### 1. Research Phase
**Where to find them:**
- **Product Hunt leaderboards**: Check monthly/yearly top hunters
- **Successful product pages**: See who hunted products similar to yours
- **Twitter**: Many top hunters are active on Twitter
- **LinkedIn**: Professional network connections

**Research their style:**
- What types of products do they hunt?
- How often do they hunt?
- What's their engagement like?
- Do they focus on specific niches?

### 2. Outreach Strategy

**❌ DON'T:**
- Cold email asking them to hunt your product immediately
- Send generic mass messages
- Offer money (against PH rules and looks bad)
- Be pushy or entitled

**✅ DO:**
- Build a relationship first
- Engage with their content genuinely
- Provide value before asking for anything
- Make a compelling case for why your product matters

### 3. Sample Outreach Message

```
Subject: Viral Video Factory - AI tool for faceless content creators

Hi [Hunter Name],

I've been following your hunts on Product Hunt and really admire your eye for innovative AI tools. Your hunt of [specific product] really resonated with me.

I'm the founder of Viral Video Factory, an AI-powered platform that helps creators make viral videos without showing their face. We've just completed our beta and have some impressive early results:

- 500+ videos created in beta
- Average 2.5M views per video
- Used by creators with 10K-1M+ followers

The product solves a real problem in the creator economy - many people want to create content but are camera-shy. Our AI handles everything from script generation to voiceover to video assembly.

I'd love to get your thoughts on the product and see if it might be something you'd consider hunting when we're ready to launch. Happy to give you early access to try it out.

No pressure at all - I know you get many requests. Just thought it might align with the types of innovative tools you typically support.

Best regards,
[Your name]
[Link to demo or early access]
```

### 4. Building Relationships

**Timeline: 2-4 weeks before launch**

**Week 1-2: Engagement**
- Follow them on Twitter/LinkedIn
- Like and comment on their posts genuinely
- Share their content when relevant
- Engage with products they hunt

**Week 3: Initial contact**
- Send thoughtful outreach message
- Offer early access to your product
- Ask for feedback, not hunting commitment

**Week 4: Follow-up**
- Share any improvements based on their feedback
- Provide social proof (user testimonials, metrics)
- Make the hunting request if relationship is good

## Alternative Strategies

### 1. **Mutual Connections**
- Ask your network if they know any top hunters
- Warm introductions are much more effective
- Leverage LinkedIn connections

### 2. **Hunter Communities**
- Join Product Hunt maker communities
- Participate in hunter Discord servers
- Attend PH meetups (virtual/in-person)

### 3. **Reciprocal Hunting**
- Offer to hunt other makers' products
- Build relationships within the maker community
- Create mutual support network

### 4. **Backup Hunters**
- Always have 2-3 hunter options
- Top choice might be busy on your launch day
- Good hunters can still deliver solid results

## Red Flags to Avoid

### ❌ **Bad Hunters:**
- Ask for money to hunt your product
- Have fake followers or engagement
- Hunt too many products (daily hunters often have less impact)
- Don't engage with the community beyond hunting
- Have history of hunting low-quality products

### ❌ **Scam Services:**
- "Guaranteed #1 Product Hunt ranking"
- "Buy upvotes" services
- "Hunter networks" that charge fees
- Fake engagement services

### Good Hunter Performance:
- **Immediate boost**: 50-100 upvotes in first hour
- **Network effect**: Comments from their followers
- **Sustained growth**: Steady upvotes throughout the day

### Great Hunter Performance:
- **Strong start**: 100-200 upvotes in first hour
- **High engagement**: 20+ comments from quality users
- **Momentum**: Trending in top 5 by midday
